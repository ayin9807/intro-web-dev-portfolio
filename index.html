cs bio

  • i was introduced to computers at a very young age, around 7 or so. my mom was getting a new laptop at work, so she retired her old one to me. i mainly used windows computers until freshman year of high school, when my parents bought me a macbook air. i was against switching from windows at first, but i'm so comfortable with using macs now that i'm now on my second macbook.
  • my mom is a software engineer, so i've been exposed to the field for a long time. however, i never really thought about majoring in computer science until i came to duke. before college, i thought about studying maybe math or bme with east asian studies. then i took cs 201, which i had a love-hate relationship with, but ultimately decided to major. currently, i'm confused about what exactly i want to do with this degree but mainly just trying to live my best (and least stressful) life. probably either going to work in corporate tech for a few years and then go to medical school in the future.
  • 50% of the time, i use a computer for entertainment, so like watching tv or youtube. 40% for school work, so coding, writing papers, or googling things. 10% for online shopping and other miscellaneous tasks.
  • depending on how one defines "computer scientist," i envision the work to be a combination of programming, algorithms/systems/AI/other research, and teaching.
  • i am taking this course because:
    1. i want to become more proficient in html, css, and javascript.
    2. i want to become more familiar with how to build web apps.
    3. i want an excuse to build a personal website.
    4. i needed a cs elective.
  • not very familiar with building websites (why i'm taking this class), but i assume one needs to understand some combination of ui/ux, how to incorporate databases, and html/css/javascript to build a good website.
